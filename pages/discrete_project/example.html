MASH-1 is secure becasue when the factors of N are unknown, preimage and collision attacks require 2n/2 and 2n/4 operations. This is no better than brute force attacks.

This security comes from the fact that N only has four factors: itself, the two prime factors, and 1. A prime number is defined as an integer with only factors itself and 1. Our modulus is the product of two primes so we know off the bat that those primes are factors in addition to itself and 1. We can also know that there are no other factors because none of the known factors have any additional factors. For example 12=3*4 which we can rewrite to 12=3*2*2=6*2 revealing factors 6 and 2 from 3 and 4. For products of large primes, finding the only two factors is computationally very expensive and take a lopt of time.

Hash algorithms affect climate change becasue they can improve storage efficiency which is one of the biggest costs of the digital age. Storing 100gb of data in the cloud results in about 0.2 tons of CO2. Reducing the size of data we need to store can have a big impact on the carbon footprint of many cloud services.
